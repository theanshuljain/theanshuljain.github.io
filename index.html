What is this Web Site all about?

This web site is devoted to my work in Mathematics, Physics, and Engineering Education! My goal in creating this site is to share my teaching experience, research interests, and passion for making complex concepts accessible to students around the world. It is also my dream to help inspire the next generation of engineers and scientists.

I am currently a Graduate Student in Aerospace Engineering Sciences at the University of Colorado, Boulder. Since 2023, I've been working as a Teaching Assistant, guiding students through laboratory experiments and helping them develop a deep understanding of biological and physical principles. Over this time, I've kept detailed lesson plans, carefully logged student progress, and continuously refined my teaching methods.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe in making education accessible, engaging, and transformative through clear explanations and real-world relevance. My approach centers on three key principles:

Mathematics Made Simple
Transforming abstract mathematical concepts into visual, understandable lessons that build strong foundations.

Hands-on Learning
Creating engaging laboratory experiences that connect theory to practice, making learning memorable and meaningful.

Student-Centered Approach
Adapting teaching methods to diverse learning styles and needs, ensuring every student can succeed.

• Encouraged teamwork and scientific reasoning by fostering a collaborative environment focused on applying physics to practical engineering problems
